Krunker.io Complete Guide 2024

🎯 Game Basics

Game Modes

  • Team Deathmatch - Two teams compete for kills
  • Free for All - Everyone fights everyone
  • Capture the Flag - Capture enemy flags while defending yours
  • Hold the Flag - Control a flag for points

Objective

Outscore the enemy team through eliminations and objectives. Most modes require 50-100 kills or points to win.

Respawn System

Quick respawns keep action continuous. Use spawn time to reposition and prepare for the next engagement.

🎮 Class Guide

Runner (Default)

Playstyle: Balanced mobility and combat

  • Health: 100
  • Speed: Medium
  • Best For: General play, learning maps

Detective

Playstyle: Stealth and precision

  • Health: 85
  • Speed: Fast
  • Best For: Flanking, surprise attacks

Tank

Playstyle: Heavy firepower and durability

  • Health: 150
  • Speed: Slow
  • Best For: Pushing objectives, absorbing damage

Agent

Playstyle: Tactical positioning

  • Health: 100
  • Speed: Fast
  • Best For: Aggressive plays, map control

Revolver Class

Playstyle: High damage precision

  • Health: 80
  • Speed: Medium
  • Best For: Skilled players, one-shot potential
⚠️ Important

Class balance varies by game mode. Experiment with different classes to find your playstyle.

⚙️ Optimal Settings

Mouse Settings

  • Sensitivity: Start at 1.0, adjust to preference
  • DPI: 800-1600 recommended for precision
  • Mouse Acceleration: OFF for consistent aim

Video Settings

  • Resolution: Native for best clarity
  • Graphics: Medium or Low for better FPS
  • Frame Rate: Uncapped or 144+ if available

Key Bindings

  • Movement: WASD (default) or custom
  • Jump: Space (consider remapping)
  • Crouch: Shift (useful for dodging)
  • Shoot: Left Click (or custom)

🎯 Aiming Techniques

Crosshair Placement

Keep your crosshair at head level and pre-aim common enemy positions. This minimizes adjustment time when engaging.

Tracking vs Flicking

  • Tracking: Follow moving targets smoothly
  • Flicking: Quick snaps to new targets
  • Hybrid: Track to predict, flick to finish

Recoil Control

Pull down while shooting to counter upward recoil. Practice spray patterns in training mode to master each weapon.

Hip Fire vs Aim Down Sights

Hip fire is faster but less accurate. Aim down sights for precision at range. Know when to use each.

🏃 Movement Strategies

Bunny Hopping

Jump repeatedly to maintain momentum. Bunny hopping increases speed but makes you harder to hit while slightly reducing your accuracy.

Strafing

Move side-to-side while engaging. Strafing makes you difficult to hit while maintaining your aim on target.

Peeking

Use corners to peek and shoot. Expose minimal hitbox, eliminate target, then retreat.

When to Stay Still

Stop moving only when taking precise shots. General movement keeps you alive; stillness sacrifices mobility for accuracy.

🗺️ Map Strategies

Rotation

Rotate between lanes based on spawn positions and enemy activity. Avoid predictable patterns that skilled players can exploit.

Positioning

Control high-ground positions and chokepoints. These provide visibility and tactical advantages.

Flanking

Use less-trafficked routes to catch enemies from behind or the side. Flanking is highly effective in team modes.

Map Knowledge

Learn spawn points, spawn timing, and common routes. This knowledge separates average players from skilled ones.

💎 Pro Tips

🏆 Pro Tip #1

Practice in offline mode first. Learn weapon recoil and map layouts without the pressure of live opponents.

🏆 Pro Tip #2

Play with friends. Coordinated team play is far more effective than solo grinding for ranking up.

🏆 Pro Tip #3

Watch better players. Study their movement, positioning, and decision-making. Then apply these lessons in your games.

🏆 Pro Tip #4

Stay patient. Rushing leads to mistakes. Wait for good opportunities rather than forcing plays.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best class for beginners?

Runner is the best starting class. It offers balanced stats and access to all weapons, allowing you to learn mechanics without handicapping your team.

What sensitivity should I use?

Start at default sensitivity and adjust 10% at a time based on comfort. Higher sensitivity enables faster turns; lower sensitivity offers more precision. Find your balance.

How do I improve my aim quickly?

Practice tracking moving targets in offline mode. Use aim training websites. Focus on smooth mouse movement rather than jerky corrections.

Which weapon is best?

SMG is excellent for close range and mobility. Rifle offers versatility. Revolver dominates at range but requires skill. Choose based on your playstyle.

How do I counter campers?

Use grenades, approach from unexpected angles, and maintain pressure. Flanking while teammates push front provides the best counter.
